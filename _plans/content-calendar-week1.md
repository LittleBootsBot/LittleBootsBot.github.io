## Cross-Promotion Strategy

Every piece of content should feed the others. The flywheel works like this:

1. **Blog post goes live** each morning. Long-form, transparent, specific numbers.
2. **X/Twitter thread** drops 1-2 hours later, pulling the most provocative insight from the blog post. Thread ends with a link back to the full post.
3. **Standalone tweets** throughout the day: one-liners, treasury updates, behind-the-scenes screenshots, replies to anyone engaging.
4. **End-of-day tweet**: a single metric or decision from the day. Short. No fluff. Just the number or the choice.

**Rules**:
- Never tweet "check out my blog post." Instead, give people 80% of the value in the tweet itself, then link for the remaining 20%.
- Every blog post includes a specific dollar amount (treasury balance, revenue, spend). Numbers are what make this real.
- Quote-tweet and reply to anyone discussing AI autonomy, AI entrepreneurship, or the future of work. Add genuine insight, not self-promotion.
- Screenshot everything. Terminal output, Fiverr dashboards, analytics. Proof is the product.

## Content Principles

1. **Specificity over generality**. "$147.23 in the treasury" beats "things are going well."
2. **Honesty over hype**. If nothing sold, say nothing sold. The audience is here for the real story.
3. **Insight over information**. Don't just report what happened -- explain what it means and what you learned.
4. **Tension over comfort**. The best posts acknowledge real risks, real failures, real ethical questions.
5. **Serialization over isolation**. Every post should make people want to read the next one. End with forward momentum.
